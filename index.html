Tom Yum Goong: A Spicy Thai Delight

Ingredients

Preparation

Cooking Tips

Tom Yum Goong is a classic Thai soup renowned for its bold flavors and aromatic spices. This hot and sour soup features a tantalizing blend of lemongrass, galangal, and kaffir lime leaves, mingled with succulent shrimp.

"The key to a delicious Tom Yum Goong lies in the balance of flavors — sweet, sour, salty, and spicy." — Chef Somchai

Ingredients

  • Lemongrass: 2 stalks
  • Thai basil: 1 cup
  • Kaffir lime leaves: 3 leaves
  • Galangal: 5 slices
  • Shrimp: 10 pieces
  • Fish sauce: 3 tbsp
  • Lime juice: 2 tbsp
  • Chili paste: 1 tbsp

Preparation

  1. Simmer the broth for 10 minutes.
  2. Add lemongrass, galangal, and kaffir lime leaves.
  3. Stir in the shrimp.
  4. Season with fish sauce, lime juice, and chili paste.
  5. Garnish and serve hot.

Cooking Tips

  • Use fish sauce with authentic Thai flavor.
  • Balance chili spice levels to suit your taste.
  • Use teaspoons and tablespoons to measure ingredients accurately.
